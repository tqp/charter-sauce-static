The Exumas: New Year's 2022

After a year hiatus, we've got another great New Year's week sail planned with Captain Pete and Custom Yacht Charter. This year, we'll be exploring The Exumas, a chain of more than 300 islands in The Bahamas, southeast of Nassau. The Exumas have so much to offer, from the famous Pig Beach, to sunken remnants of the drug cartels of the 1980's.

The sail will start from Staniel Cay, an island 75 miles from Nassau. From there, we'll slowly make our way up the northern half of the island chain to Nassau. We'll spend New Year's Eve at the Staniel Cay Yacht Club's famous masquerade party. Like all our trips, we'll be sailing the boat ourselves, at the direction of Captain Pete.

Day 1 : Arrive on Staniel Cay

Wednesday, December 29th
The crew will fly into Staniel Cay, a scenic island about halfway down the The Exumas island chain, 75 miles south of Nassau. From the airport, you'll make your way to the Staniel Cay Yacht Club, where Captain Pete will have the boat waiting for us.

Everyone can unpack and start getting acclimated to life at sea. Captain Pete will conduct an orientation and safety briefing. After that, we will head to the Staniel Cay Yacht club for welcome drinks and a chance to get to know (or catch up with) your crew mates.

The Staniel Cay Yacht Club will have events going on all day long, from a "long-drive" golf contest in the morning to their annual costume Pirate Party that night.

Overnight Location:
Staniel Cay

Day 2 : Black Point

Thursday, December 30th
We'll cook breakfast on the boat before taking the boat for a sail around the area. We may head down to Black Point on Great Guana Cay to check out the secluded beaches there, or we'll head north to check out some of the islands in that direction. One way or another, this is the day where you'll get your sea-legs.

If we find a nice cove to spend the night, we might just anchor there. Otherwise, we'll head back to Staniel Cay and get the boat situated for New Year's Eve.

Sailing Time:
3 Hours
Overnight Location:
TBD: Anchorage or Staniel Cay

Day 3 : New Year's Eve

Friday, December 31st
We'll spend the day exploring the area around Staniel Cay. You can tour the island, catch some sun at the beach, or even snorklel the wreck of a crashed drug plane.

We'll dinghy over to Thunderball Grotto, named after the 1965 Bond film "Thunderball". The site was used for one of the action-packed scenes in two of the Bond films, "Thunderball" and 1983’s "Never Say Never Again". After swimming around the grotto, we'll have lunch on the boat before heading to Pig Beach.

Pig Beach is one of the most unique places in The Bahamas. The pigs aren't native to the island, and while there are many theories and folktales about it, nobody really knows how the pigs got there. Wherever they came from, there are now about 20 pigs and piglets living the easy life, with daily visits from Bahamians and tourists.

We'll all meet back at the boat to get dressed for the evening - and to have a champagne toast with the crew to kick off our New Year's Eve fun. Around sunset, we'll dinghy to the Staniel Cay Yacht Club for their annual Masquerade Party.

Overnight Location:
Staniel Cay

Day 4 : Swim With Sharks on Compass Cay

Saturday, January 1st
We'll get a late start on New Year's Day and make the 2-hour sail northward to Compass Cay. There, we'll swim with nurse sharks at the Compass Cay Marina. Nurse Sharks are a type of shark indigenous to the Bahamas Waters and for the most part harmless to humans.

Those of us that survive swimming with the sharks will continue sailing northward past several idyllic, uninhabited islands. This will be the perfect day to hone our sailing skills, tacking and jibing our way up to Shroud Cays and the Exuma Cays Land and Sea Park.

Sailing Time:
4 Hours
Overnight Location:
Shroud Cays

Day 5 : Exuma Cays Land and Sea Park

Sunday, January 2nd
Established in 1958, the Exuma Cays Land and Sea Park is the first marine reserve in The Bahamas and the wider Caribbean. Covering 112,640 acres of land and sea, it is renowned for its breathtaking beauty, species biodiversity, secluded beaches, amazing views and safe anchorages. Throughout the park you can explore the underwater magnificence of coral reefs, mangroves and seagrasses, stroll along pristine beaches, traverse several hiking trails, and observe tons of wildlife including birds, fish, turtles and iguanas.

Depending on how the crew is feeling, we can spend the night in the park, or we can make the four-hour sail to Norman Cay for dinner and some local night life.

Sailing Time:
4 Hours
Overnight Location:
Shroud Cays

Day 6 : Norman's Cay

Monday, January 3rd
Norman Cay served as the headquarters for Carlos Lehder's drug-smuggling operation from 1978 until around 1982. It was a drug smuggling hub and tropical hideaway for the Medellin cartel. Back in the 70’s the Medellin Cartel used Norman’s Cay as a drug smuggling base where planes would take off full of cocaine into the US.

There are lots of options for this day. From exploring Emerald Rock to snorkeling the wreck of one of Pablo Escobar's drug planes that crashed off the coast here in 1980.

The real highlight on the island for us will be MacDuff's, Beach Bar & Grill. For those craving a nice meal, the restaurant at MacDuff's resort is known to be one of the best in the Bahamas.

Sailing Time:
4 Hours
Overnight Location:
Norman's Cay

Day 7 : Sail to Nassau

Tuesday, January 4th
After a leisurely morning on Norman's Cay, we'll head out on our longest sail of the trip, making our way to Nassau for our last night on the boat.

There will be lots of options for dinner in Nassau. Or, we can stay on the boat. Either way, the esteemed Awards Ceremony will take place just after sunset in a place to be determined.

Sailing Time:
6 Hours
Overnight Location:
Palm Cay Marina, Nassau

Day 8 : Disembark in Nassau

Wednesday, January 5th
We'll be up bright and early to pack our things and leave the boat.

All cabins have a private bathroom and independent shower, as well as incomparable natural lighting thanks to the fixed hull windows, deck hatches and portholes that open out to the hull-side or to the cockpit.

Cabin Options:
The Lagoon 52 has a variety of cabin sizes and layouts. From spacious cabins with direct deck access to single-person bunk beds. Prices for cabins vary according to the size and amenities. Unlike many sailboats, every cabin has its own head and shower.

It's assumed that the non-bunk cabins will accommodate two people, but on previous trips, people wanting a bit more space for themselves have booked the entire cabin for themselves. The two-person cabins must be booked as a whole, meaning, you can't book one of the places in a 2-person cabin without also booking the second spot at the same time.

A 50% deposit will be required to reserve your cabin, and your spot on the trip.

Cabins A & B:
The two rear cabins (A & B) are the largest on the boat and have direct access to the aft deck through "Lamborghini-style" doors. Since the crew spends the majority of the time on the boat congregating on the aft deck, these tend to be the most social cabins.

Cabin A is the second-largest cabin on the boat and it has the largest shower. With its door opening directly into the aft deck salon area, this tends to be the most social cabin on the boat. Cabin B is the most spacious cabin on the boat and, with its private entrance, it also offers the most privacy.
Cabins D & E:
The two front cabins (D & E) have long windows, allowing lots of light in during the day. The lofted beds are positioned in the center of the cabin allowing easy access from either side of the room.

Cabin C:
Cabin C is the smallest cabin on the boat and has less closet space than the other cabins. It works well for a couple who doesn't mind cozying-up with each other at night, or for a single person who wants to have a whole cabin to themselves.

The Bunk Beds:
For those who don't plan to spend a lot of time in their cabin, or who plan to sleep above deck under the stars, a bunk bed might be the right option for you.

Getting There

Since we are doing a one-way sail, crew members should plan to fly into Staniel Cay Airport (TYM) and fly out of Nassau (NAS). Since Staniel Cay is a tiny little airport in the middle of nowhere, you're going to need to book a flight on one of the airlines that serves that airport.

Makers Air has daily flights into Staniel Cay Fort Lauderdale, Florida for around $350. Flamingo Air has daily flights from Nassau to Staniel Cay for around $150. Titan air has the same flight from Nassau for around $115.
